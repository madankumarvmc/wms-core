WMS Core — User Manual

WMS Core is the handheld (phone/scanner) app and supervisor console for truck loading and bin inventory. This guide covers the two handheld tasks — Loading and Inventory Count — and the Supervisor Console.

1 · Overview

There are two places you'll work:

🚚 Handheld app

Opens at /wms-loading on a phone or scanner (HHT). Operators load trucks and count inventory. Works offline.

🖥️ Supervisor console

Opens at /wms-console on a desktop. Supervisors track trucks, review SAP sync, and import data.

The handheld has two tasks, controlled by your role:

Your roleWhat you see
Loading OperatorLoading only
Inventory RecorderInventory Count only
Loading Supervisor / both rolesA task picker to choose either

2 · Getting started

Opening & installing the app

  1. Open /wms-loading in the phone browser and log in with your WMS Core account.
  2. Use your browser's Add to Home Screen so it launches like a native app (full screen, offline-ready).

The top bar

Every screen shows a status bar:

  • ONLINE / OFFLINE — your connection. The app keeps working offline.
  • N queued — actions saved on the phone waiting to sync (appears only when offline work is pending).
  • ⇄ Task — switch between Loading and Inventory Count (only if you have both).

The task picker

If you can do both tasks, you land on the picker. Tap a tile to start; the app remembers your choice next time (switch anytime with ⇄ Task).

3 · Loading a truck

The Loading home shows today's numbers, the trucks still To Load (oldest first), and trucks Loaded Today. Scan or type a truck at the top, or tap one from the list.

3.1 Find & claim a truck

Scan the truck barcode (or type it and tap →). Matching open trucks appear — tap one to start loading.

If a truck is already claimed by another operator, you'll be asked to confirm a takeover before continuing — the change is logged.

3.2 Scan & confirm coils

The loading screen shows a progress bar and running counts (Loaded / Pending / Skipped / Total), then the coil list. Scan a coil — its card appears; tap Confirm Coil Load to load it. A scanner that sends the code fast is detected automatically; manual typists tap the orange →.

3.3 Undo & over-scan

Undo — tap Undo on a loaded coil to send it back to pending (within the undo window) if you scanned it by mistake.

Over-scan — if you scan a coil that isn't on the truck's plan and over-scan is allowed, you can add and load it; it's recorded as an unplanned coil.

3.4 Complete the truck

When every coil is loaded, tap Complete Loading. This finalises the truck and triggers the SAP goods-issue. Completing needs a connection.

Completed trucks appear under Loaded Today on the home screen. Tap one to see a read-only summary of what was loaded.

3.5 Short-load (finishing with coils left)

If you must finish before every coil is loaded, tap Complete Loading — you'll be asked for a reason for each remaining coil, then a supervisor enters their PIN to approve. Approval is online only.

3.6 Working offline

Scanning and confirming keep working with no signal. Each action is saved on the phone (the N queued badge shows how many) and syncs automatically when you're back online. You never lose work.

Completing a truck and short-load approvals need a connection — reconnect before finishing.

4 · Inventory Count

Inventory Count maps which coil is in which bin. You walk to a bin, scan its location barcode, then scan each coil in it. Every scan is saved immediately — there's no separate "save each coil" step.

Scan a bin

Scan (or type) the bin's location barcode to open it.

Scan the coils

Scan each coil in the bin. Each one appears at the top of the list and the running count updates. The app tells you if a coil moved from another bin or was already here.

  • QC pill — every coil defaults to OK. Tap it to mark a QC hold; tap again to clear.
  • ✕ — removes a coil you scanned by mistake.
  • Save & next bin — finish this bin and go straight to scanning the next one.
Inventory Count works offline too — scans queue and sync just like loading. Reopening a bin shows the coils already recorded in it.

5 · Supervisor Console

The console (/wms-console, desktop) is for Loading Supervisors. The left menu switches between views.

5.1 Dashboard

Live counts (open/active trucks, awaiting approval, GI status) and throughput over a date range.

5.2 Trucks

Search and filter every truck/plan. Click a row to open its detail drawer: coils and their status, the full event history, and per-coil SAP T1 status. From here a supervisor can complete, cancel, or reopen a plan and retry a failed SAP push.

5.3 Inventory

The current coil→bin map (from the push feed, uploads, and in-app Inventory Counts). Search by coil or bin.

5.4 SAP views

Three views track the SAP integration:

  • SAP Confirmations — outbound goods-issue (GI) per truck. Held partial loads can be Released; failures Retried.
  • SAP Loading — the per-coil T1 loading push. Sent = accepted by SAP, Failed = rejected (retry after fixing), Disabled = loaded while push was off (turn it on, then push).
  • SAP Log — a timeline of every SAP call in and out, with result and message.

5.5 Import (fallback)

If the automatic feed is unavailable, upload a CSV/XLSX to create Loading Plans or Bin Inventory. Download the template, upload your file, map the columns, preview for errors, then commit.

6 · Troubleshooting

ProblemWhat to do
Scanning doesn't open the coilTap the orange → next to the box, or press Enter. On a scanner, check it's sending the barcode into the focused field.
"Not on this truck"The coil isn't on this plan. Check you're on the right truck; use over-scan only if allowed and correct.
"Already loaded"That coil is already done — no action needed.
Can't complete the truckCoils are still pending — either load them, or use the short-load flow with a supervisor PIN.
"N queued" won't clearYou're offline or the connection dropped. Reconnect; the badge clears once everything syncs.
Count says "already counted in this bin"You scanned the same coil twice — it's counted once, nothing to fix.

7 · Glossary

TermMeaning
CoilA steel/aluminium coil, identified by its barcode.
BinA storage location, identified by a location barcode.
PlanA truck's loading list (which coils go on it), usually sent from SAP.
QC holdA quality flag on a coil — informational; it does not block work.
Short-loadCompleting a truck with some planned coils left off, with reasons and supervisor approval.
Goods issue (GI)The SAP confirmation that a truck's goods have shipped.
T1The per-coil loading transaction sent to SAP when a coil is loaded.
